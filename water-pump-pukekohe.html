Benefits of having water pump

Cleaner water supply

Water from the city line contains a lot of chemicals added to minimize the contaminants like lead, iron, and hydrogen sulfide present in the water. These contaminants are not only harmful to your health but also cause serious damage to your fixtures in the near future. With a much cleaner water supply, the water pump pushes water from the well to your taps and other fixtures to wash vehicles, clean dishes, and take showers. The fresh and tasty water from the well is far safer than the city water.

Save money

It will cost you money for the drilling of a well and installation of a water pump, but it is a quality investment beneficial for your future. By installing a water pump, you can cut down on the monthly water bill at your home like city water users and enjoy fresh water from the well free from any chemicals.

Support water demand

Having a water pump you are free to use any amount of water according to your requirements. The well water will run as efficiently as the city water ideal for every circumstance and utilized for our daily needs. If you have high water needs, you should definitely have a water pump to have a direct water source to your home.

Water pump installations

Over the past few decades, water pump technologies have drastically changed. Thus, fixing an old pump is not always worth it instead of getting a new one. The most important aspect of water pump installation is choosing the correct pump to suit the job. Many of the problematic installations or repairs we encounter are incorrectly specified. Our team of proficient plumbers analyzes the situation and installs the appropriate water pump according to the demands of your water system.

Signs of faulty water pump

Strange noises

Water may sputter through faucets before flowing normally if your water system has excess air trapped in it. Even though this won't affect your health, it still counts as a contaminant, since it's something that comes through the air that you don't want to be there. This issue mainly occurs due to a change in the water table, damage to your good pump, or an issue with your drop pipe. In worst cases, the well requires drilling to fix the issue. So it is a warning sign that needs your attention.

Change in water quality

A bad smell or taste in your water is a clear indication that something’s wrong with your water pump or well water. It's possible that the issue isn't always at the microscopic level. The water contaminants present in the groundwater like iron, manganese, hydrogen sulfide, and other chemicals make way to the well degrading the water quality and making it unsafe for use. If you suspect that there are some sort of contaminants or sediments present in your water, make sure you get it tested by a specialist to safeguard your health and give you peace of mind.

Pumping sand or sediment

Sand or sediment in the well water is a common problem encountered by the majority of house owners. If you have an older well, sediments can pile up at the bottom of the well from the bedrock. A well pump is usually situated 10 feet or more above the well's bottom, but the well can silt in overtime, adding sand and silt to the bottom. Sand and silt are particularly difficult for the pump to handle. When sand gets pumped, it causes wear and tears in the equipment, clogging and replacement of its fixture or the whole unit itself. If sand or silt is identified in the water, the core issue should be addressed as soon as possible.

Declined water pressure

Reduced water pressure in your taps and other fixtures can be equally irritating as well as time-consuming and make chores inefficient. This might be an indication of a malfunctioning well pump, but it could also be a sign of clogged valves or a failing pressure tank. If your water contains a lot of iron, your pump may become blocked with iron germs. This problem may be avoided with a distinctive cleaning process that removes the iron accumulated in the pump.

The water pump cycles on and off constantly

If you find your water pump running a lot more often than it normally does, it might be a sign of a larger problem. Sometimes it may be due to the malfunctioning of check valves. The basic function of the check value is to stop water from flowing back into the well from the water pump. Therefore, when the valve stops working properly, the water goes back into the well causing the switch to continuously activate the pump.
