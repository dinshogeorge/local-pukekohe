Drain Unblocking Services in Pukekohe

Blocked drains are always a major cause of worry in every household. With a minor problem, Sometimes it will take only a few minutes to get everything cleaned and drains cleared. But if there’s a major issue like tree root invasion in the drain pipes you will definitely need a drain specialist to diagnose the issue and resolve it as efficiently as possible. For a quick and easy fix for your clogged drains, feel free to contact us and make an appointment with our efficient plumbers.

Drain repair and replacement services

Here, at Pukekohe Plumbers, we handle all kinds of drain repair and replacement services with the use of modern equipment and technology available.

CCTV camera drain inspection

CCTV drain inspection is one of the advanced technologies we employ to inspect and identify any potential issues like leaks or clogging in the drain pipes. Our specialized plumbers quickly find the leaks by inserting high-tech CCTV cameras into the drainage system which provides a live feed of the sewer lines.

Drain Jetting

As the primary objective of the drain jetting is to remove the blockage due to hair, dirt, and other substances, jet sprays can be used to clean the inside of the drainage system. The high-pressure water through the nozzle clears all the obstructions in the line. So drain jetting not only clears the debris that causes clogging in the drains, but also serves as an effective preventive measure. Hire our professionals to get the system cleaned in no time.

Tree root cutting

Tree roots pose a major threat to the drainage system as they may get attracted to the water source that provides better grounds to grow. The roots invade the tiny holes that already exist in the pipe and make the holes even bigger over time. If there are any trees growing near your area, you must call us to have your drain pipes checked by us to prevent further damage to your system.

Drain relining

Drain relining is an eco-friendly solution to cracked and damaged pipes. By drain relining, an extra coating of metal is inserted into the damaged pipes which act as a protective layer in the drain. The coating is hardened using UV lights or heat to safeguard the inside of the existing pipe.

Common causes of Drain blockage

Dirt and Soap

Dirt can contribute to drain blockage when you wash off excessive amounts of dirt down the drain. Also, soaps leave fatty residues known as soap scum which leads to slow draining of water. They also combine with hair and dirt to form clogs in the drains.

Food waste

Dumping food waste can cause blocks in the pipelines, especially oily foods. These substances have sticky properties that make them attached to the pipes down the sink, gradually clogging the lines.

Hair

Hair is a common reason for blockage in every household which can be avoided to some extent by regularly cleaning the plughole. However, the build-up of hair that escapes through shower drains can cause clogging in the drains.

Flushing too much toilet paper

Despite being easily soluble in water, toilet paper when flushed all at once can contribute to clogging the drains. Although it may cause only a temporary blockage, sometimes it may lead to major issues in the drain or sewer lines.

Hard water

Hard water contains a significant amount of calcium and minerals in it. When used for a long time minerals and calcium present in the water leaves white residues that pile up in the sewer lines causing obstruction in the system.

Old pipelines

Old pipeworks are prone to develop rust on their surface after a period of time. Due to corrosion, these pipes tend to form tiny pinholes later developing into bigger ones which may lead to sediment and sand entering the pipeline and cause major clogging in the drainage system.

Flushing non-flushable items

The non-flushable items like sanitary napkins, diapers, dental floss, and toys when flushed can form a blockage in the drainage system. Although it may not cause sudden clogging, over time these things obstruct the free flow of water leading to the slow draining of water in the toilet.

Tree roots

Growing tree roots invade the drainage or other underground pipes as they are attracted to the water source which aids in the growth of trees. Tree roots open up the existing cracks in the pipe which may cause the sand and sediment to accumulate causing a severe blockage in the drainage system.
