Benefits of having water filter for your family

Healthy and safe

By installing water purifiers you can have clean water free from impurities and contaminants like chlorine and lead that are harmful to your health. Drinking filtered water is an added advantage to both your health and your lifestyle. It helps to boost your immune system and reduce the risk of diseases like cancer.

Eco-friendly

The most serious issue with the booming bottled water sector is that it is causing serious environmental damage. When you have mineral-rich, clean drinking water on hand in your kitchen, you won't have to run to the store to stock up on bottled water or reach for the awful tap water when you're thirsty.

Cost-effective

With an unlimited source of filtered water available at your home, you don’t have to waste money on bottled drinking water at all. Filtered water not only saves you money but also provides you the same refreshing taste as bottled water, right from your faucet.

Odorless and tasty

By removing impurities and other contaminants, water filter systems eliminate bad odor and preserve minerals in it making the tap water taste better.

Signs of faulty water filter

Foul odor

An unpleasant smell from the purified water indicates that there is something wrong with your water filter. To stop this, you will need to replace or install a new one to enjoy fresh and clean water again.

Low water pressure

The decline in the water pressure shows that your water filter has developed some blockage which prevents the free flow of water from the filter to the taps. Low water pressure can also be caused due to leaks or clogging in other pipes or fixtures at your home.

Warning lights

Some water filters have warning lights on the outside of the water filter to show whether the system isn't working properly or its filter needs to be replaced. So, have it checked and fixed as soon as it displays the warning lights.

Slow filtering speed

Slow speed in filtering water can be very frustrating at times when you are very thirsty and in need of water so badly. When it takes unusually long to have the water filtered, it means that there is some kind of clogging in the system which needs to be cleared to regain its filtering speed.

Bad taste

The bad taste of water is a clear sign of a failing water filter. So when the filter starts to fail, there may be a decline in the quality of the filtered water. If you notice a metallic or salty taste, it is because of the hard water. Having a water softener can cause the water to taste salty by adding too much sodium to counteract hard water minerals.

Noise from fixtures

When the system doesn't work properly, the other fixtures tend to make strange noises. This is a common indication of reverse osmosis filters failing. If it keeps on increasing, you need to seek immediate help from professionals.
